Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Thermometers were found in all coolers. Temperatures were being recorded on a daily basis. Logs were present for verification purposes.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Microbiological

Sushi rice was prepared and stored in a rice cooker. Do not store for greater than 2 hours, at room temperature. Seaweed, used for sushi rice, was not stored on top of the preparation cooler. Recommend placing a plexi-glass in the front to act as a sneeze guard. Scoop was placed in a separate container, outside of the ice.

 0 3

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

All food items, used for sush, such as fish roe, immitation crab and other items were stored in the cooler, at the time of the inspection. Continue to use the following tips: 1. Do not stack inserts. 2. Use metal inserts for potentially hazardous foods. 3. Use probe thermometers for monitoring food temperatures. 4. Keep large lid closed as much as possible, and cover potentially hazardous foods with individual lids. 5. Service the cooler so that the bottom portion is storing food as close to 4 C without freezing. 6. Do not overfill inserts.

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

No fish products are permitted to be stored at room temperature. Salmon was being prepared, at the time of the inspection. It must be stored in the cooler or freezer at the approved temperatures.

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Bamboo matting used for the sushi must be covered with saran wrap at all times. This product is difficult to sanitize in the dishwasher, therefore it is vital that the required steps are taken.

Food Not Protected - General

Most foods were stored a minimum of 6" off the floor. However, there was a bin of dried nuts, which were for staff use. Again, ensure all foods, including those for staff consumption are not stored directly on the floor.

All foods must be stored a minimum of 6" or 15 cm off the floor.

Improper Sanitary Facilities

Handsinks were free of dishes and unobstructed, ensure all staff use these sinks for handwashing purposes. Ensure handwashing occurs after every chance of possible contamination using the correct handwashing technique: 1. Wet hands, 2. Apply liquid soap, 3. Lather for 30 seconds, 4. Rinse, 5. Dry using paper towel. Use towel to turn off taps.

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

Both coolers were cleaned and sanitized, at the time of the inspection. The two compartment sink must be free of any dishes and clutter. Use this sink for preparation purposes for the sushi. The front, right side storage was more organized and non-food items were removed from here. The fan, found in the back kitchen, was clean and sanitary.

OUTSTANDING The toaster oven was caked in grease and food debris. Remove all aluminum foil and degrease thoroughly. Use this apparatus for the storage of the tempura.
