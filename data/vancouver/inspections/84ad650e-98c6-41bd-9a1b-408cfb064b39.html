Lack of/Inadequate Food Safety Plan

Monitor AND Record the temperatures of you coolers two times daily. Ensure that all coolers have accurate thermometers. The Food Premises Regulation (B.C. Reg. 210/99) section 23 requires that: "every operator of a food service establishment must have written procedures to ensure that a health hazard does not occur in the operation of the establishment."

Food Safety Plan monitoring AND recording of cooler temperatures is not occurring.

Food Not Protected From Contamination - Other Sources

>Non food grade containers were not observed to be holding food at the time of the inspection. >Chemicals were not observed to be held beside food in the rear storage/boiler room. >Newsprint was again observed to be used to cover vegetables or to line produce containers. Newspaper is not considered to be clean and must not be allowed to contact food.

 1 0

Food Handler Illness/Hygiene/Habits

>Cross contamination between dirty dishes and clean ware was not observed. Dishwashing was not occurring at the time of the inspection. The owner was reminded that clean dishes must always be handled with clean sanitary gloves or hands. Ensure that there is one pair of gloves available for dirty dishes, and other clean gloves available for clean dishes. If bare hands are used to touch clean dishware, hands must first be properly washed at a hand washing station.

 1 1

Inadequate Cooling and/or Refrigerated Storage of Potentially Hazardous Food(s)

Large plastic containers of raw meat were not observed to be held at room temperature. However, at the time of the inspection (2:20 pm) many containers of potentially hazardous food were observed to be held un-refrigerated in the kitchen cooking area (spring rolls, raw egg, tofu, cooked meats, bean sprouts, cooked egg plant). Also, the kitchen preparation cooler lid was open and too much potentially hazardous food was being held in the inserts. Containers of potentially hazardous food were being held directly on top of food in the cooler inserts.

>Do not hold potentially hazardous food at room temperature (especially when it is not busy). >Do not stack containers of food on preparation cooler inserts. >In order to help ensure the safety of food in the inserts, please hold only small amounts of potentially hazardous foods in the inserts (especially during slow periods). In fact, when it is slow, it would be better to store hazardous foods in the cooler below. The preparation cooler lid must be closed when it is not a busy meal period. Food stored above 4 C may allow the rapid growth of pathogenic organisms which increases the likelihood of causing a food borne illness in consumers. Operator must ensure compliance with Section 14(2) of B.C. Reg. 210/99: "Every operator of food premises must ensure that potentially hazardous food is stored or displayed at a temperature of not more than 4ï¿½C". WARNING: Failure to comply with Section 14(2) of the Food Premises Regulations may result in a violation ticket being issued ($345).

Improper Cleaning, Sanitizing of Equipment and Utensils

Dishwashing was not occurring at the time of the inspeciton. Improper cleaning and sanitizing of ware was not observed.

Inadequate Insect/Rodent Control

Either keep the rear door shut tightly or install a tightly fitting screen door. Work with your pest control company to eliminate flies. Flies can contaminate the food and surfaces they land on. The operator must comply with section 26 of the Food Premises Regulations: Every operator of food premises must ensure that the premises are (a) free of pests, (b) free of conditions that lead to the harbouring or breeding of pests, and (c) protected against the entrance of pests.

Mouse droppings were not observed. A fly was observed in the kitchen area, the rear door was observed to be open and no screen door was in place to prevent pests from entering the restaurant.

 1 0

Improper Construction/Maintenance/Sanitation of Establishment

The inside of the front preparation cooler was not observed to be dirty and moldy at the time of the inspection. However, the outside of the cooler, where the cooling unit is, is contaminated with food debris that must be removed.
